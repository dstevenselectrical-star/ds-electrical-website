About Street BA16

Why clients in Street choose DS Electrical.

Street is two miles from our Glastonbury HQ — effectively our second home. Most weeks we’re in BA16 on something: a Clarks Village retail EICR, a consumer-unit upgrade on a Cranhill Road Edwardian terrace, a 7kW EV charger on a 1960s Walton Road semi.

The town has an unusually mixed property stock thanks to the Clarks family legacy. Solid Edwardian and Victorian terraces line the older streets, with inter-war and 1960s semis filling the gaps and modern estates at Merriman Park and Hindhayes. Add Clarks Village outlet shopping, the Strode Theatre and surrounding commercial units on Gravenchon Way, and you have a town where every job needs slightly different thinking.

What stays the same is how we work: fixed-price quote after a free survey, NAPIT certification and Part P notification on every notifiable job, no day rates, no surprise extras. Five-star Google reviews, £2M public liability cover, fully insured.

FAQ

Common questions from Street clients.

Do you cover Clarks Village and the rest of Street BA16?
Yes. Street is two miles from our Glastonbury base — about 10 minutes door-to-door. We cover all of BA16 including Clarks Village, Strode College, the High Street and Street Road retail strip, the Edwardian terraces around Cranhill Road and Leigh Road, and the modern estates at Merriman Park and Hindhayes. Surrounding villages — Walton, Compton Dundon, Greinton, Ashcott and Shapwick — all sit inside our 40-minute coverage radius.
How much does an EICR cost on a Street property?
EICR pricing in Street starts at £30 per circuit (single phase) for domestic properties — most three-bed terraces have 6 to 8 circuits, so a typical EICR is £180 to £240 plus VAT. Three-phase commercial properties (Clarks Village retail units, light-industrial premises on Gravenchon Way) are priced at £40 per circuit. Final price depends on board condition, accessibility and whether the building was last tested. Survey is free.
Can you install an EV charger at a 1960s Street semi?
Yes. Street has a lot of inter-war and 1960s housing stock with original 60A or 80A service heads. We commission a DNO load assessment first — many BA16 properties need a meter tail upgrade or, occasionally, a service head upgrade before we can install a 7kW charger. We use Type A RCBO protection on every EV install (mandatory under BS 7671 Amendment 2). OZEV-eligible chargers fitted, full Part P notification.
