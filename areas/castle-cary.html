About Castle Cary BA7

Why clients in Castle Cary choose DS Electrical.

Castle Cary is a picture-postcard Somerset market town — honey-stone buildings, a working High Street, an excellent station with direct trains to London Paddington, and a steady stream of London weekenders and full-time relocators. It’s a 25-minute drive from our Glastonbury HQ, and on the same A371/A359 corridor we use for Bruton, Ditcheat and Wincanton.

The properties matter here. Period stone cottages and Georgian townhouses on Bailey Hill and the High Street; large Victorian villas around North Cary; a high concentration of restored farmhouses and converted barns in the surrounding Yeo Valley; and a slow trickle of contemporary architect-led new-builds taking advantage of the Bruton-effect (Hauser & Wirth is a 5-minute drive). It’s an area where the brief is usually “exceptional, but invisible.”

Fixed-price quote after a free survey, NAPIT certification on every notifiable job, no day rates, no surprise extras. Five-star Google reviews, £2M public liability cover, fully insured for high-value period properties.

Typical work in Castle Cary

Period cottage and Georgian townhouse rewires, restored-farmhouse first and second-fix electrics, consumer-unit upgrades on rural properties with original Wylex split-load boards, discreet 7kW EV chargers chosen to complement period architecture, kitchen and bathroom electrical refurbs, smart-lighting designs (Lutron, Rako, Crestron), full CCTV systems with ColorVu cameras for outbuildings and driveways, and three-phase supplies for restored barns and equestrian properties.

Housing stock

Honey-stone Georgian townhouses, period stone cottages on Bailey Hill and Fore Street, generous Victorian villas around North Cary, restored 17th- and 18th-century farmhouses, converted stone barns (often Grade II listed), 1930s detached on the Ansford fringe, and a small but rising number of contemporary architect-led new-builds. The Paddington-line station has pulled mid-to-high-end relocators in steadily for the last decade.

FAQ

Common questions from Castle Cary clients.

Do you cover Castle Cary BA7 and the surrounding villages?
Yes. Castle Cary is a 25-minute drive from our Glastonbury base — well inside our 40-minute coverage radius. We cover all of BA7 including the High Street market quarter, Ansford, Cumnock Road, North Cary, Galhampton, Lovington and Alford. The Bruton fringe (BA10) and Yeo Valley villages — Ditcheat, Pylle, Wyke Champflower — are five to ten minutes away and we work there regularly. Castle Cary station’s Paddington link makes the area popular with London commuters.
How much does a full house rewire cost in Castle Cary?
Full rewires in BA7 typically range from £4,800 to £8,500 plus VAT depending on size, age and finish. A standard three-bed period cottage on the High Street is around £5,200; a four-bed Victorian villa around £6,800; a five-bed converted barn or restored farmhouse with smart-home wiring £8,000+. Includes new 18th-edition consumer unit, all socket and switch positions agreed with you, NAPIT certification, Part P notification and a full Electrical Installation Certificate. Survey is free.
Can you install discreet EV chargers on a period property?
Yes. We choose chargers that complement period architecture — slim profiles, anthracite or graphite finishes that read as “not there” against stone and render — and route the cable run through the conduit path that does the least damage to the building fabric. SWA-armoured cable for long driveways. We use Type A RCBO protection on every EV install (mandatory under BS 7671 Amendment 2). OZEV-eligible chargers fitted, full Part P notification.
