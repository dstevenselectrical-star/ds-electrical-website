What's included

Emergency lighting,
done to the standard.

Every install follows BS 5266-1:2016 from the design stage through to commissioning and service. We install slim addressable LED luminaires from Knightsbridge, JCC, Aurora and Eterna, certify the system on handover, and offer the monthly function test and annual 3-hour discharge test as a contracted service so the logbook stays current and your fire risk assessment file stays clean.

01 BS 5266-1 design + survey

Free site survey to map the building's escape routes, open areas and high-risk task areas. We calculate the required minimum illuminance (1 lux on the centre line of the escape route, 0.5 lux on open-area floors, 10% of normal task lighting on high-risk tasks) and produce a layout drawing showing every luminaire, EXIT sign and final exit. Drawing supplied as part of the quote so you can see exactly what's going where.

02 Slim LED luminaires & EXIT signs

Self-contained slim LED bulkheads, recessed downlights and edge-lit running-man EXIT signs from Knightsbridge, JCC, Aurora and Eterna. Lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) battery cells where available for 5-7 year service life. Surface, recessed or wall-mount as the architecture demands. IP65 rated luminaires for kitchens, plant rooms and external escape doors.

03 Self-contained vs central battery

Self-contained systems have a battery in each luminaire and are the most common choice -- simple, cost-effective, easy to add to. Best for offices, retail, hospitality and small-to-medium commercial.

Central battery (CSPS) systems run all luminaires from one centralised battery cabinet -- preferred for large premises, multi-storey buildings, hospitals and care homes where centralised testing and longer battery life justify the higher install cost. We design and install both.

04 Addressable / self-test options

Self-test luminaires automatically run their own monthly function test and annual discharge test, log the result, and flash a fault LED if anything fails -- removing the manual switch-and-stopwatch routine. Addressable systems (DALI emergency, Knightsbridge SmartTest) report each luminaire's status to a central panel and produce a printed compliance log. Ideal for large sites, multi-occupancy and premises where manual testing is impractical.

05 Cabling, containment, segregation

Final-circuit cabling to each luminaire from the same circuit as the local lighting (so a circuit failure triggers the changeover correctly), in T&E, singles in conduit, or LSZH where required. Fire-rated containment, intumescent sealing at all compartment penetrations, and proper segregation from data and other LV per BS 7671. We do not parallel emergency luminaires off random unrelated circuits -- it defeats the entire point of the system.

06 Commissioning, certification & logbook

Every install is commissioned on handover: every luminaire activated, the full 3-hour discharge test completed, EXIT sign visibility verified at every decision point, and the BS 5266-1 emergency lighting completion certificate issued. We supply a printed emergency lighting logbook with the system and pre-fill the install records. 12-month parts & labour warranty on installation.

07 Monthly + annual service contracts

BS 5266-1 requires monthly short-duration function tests and an annual 3-hour discharge test. We deliver both as a contracted service: every luminaire tested and observed, every EXIT sign confirmed, batteries verified, logbook updated, and a service certificate issued for your fire risk assessment file. If a fitting fails the test we replace it the same day where stock allows.

08 Integrates with fire alarm + EICR

Where you also have a BS 5839-1 fire alarm system we coordinate the surveys, tests and certificates so one engineer attends, one set of paperwork is issued, and the fire risk assessment file is complete. Emergency lighting is also tested as part of an EICR in any premises where it's installed -- both checks become a single visit.

Categories explained

Maintained, non-maintained,
and the duration codes.

BS 5266-1 codes a luminaire by its mode (M = maintained, NM = non-maintained) and its rated battery duration in hours. Most premises in Mid Somerset specify M3 / NM3 -- 3 hours of battery autonomy. We design the right mix for the building.

M3

Maintained, 3-hour

Always lit (uses normal lighting circuits) and stays lit on battery for 3 hours after mains failure. Used for EXIT signs in places of assembly — cinemas, theatres, restaurants, function rooms — and any escape route that is normally dim or dark. The most common EXIT sign mode.

NM3

Non-maintained, 3-hour

Off in normal use, switches on automatically when the local final circuit loses mains supply, and runs for 3 hours on battery. Used for escape route lighting in offices, warehouses, back-of-house corridors and any space lit during occupancy. The most common escape route luminaire mode.

M1 / NM1

1-hour duration

Lower-cost 1-hour autonomy, only acceptable where premises can be evacuated and re-occupied within 1 hour and the fire risk assessment supports it. Rare in practice — most commercial premises specify 3-hour. We will tell you straight if 1-hour is appropriate or if you need to upgrade.

Frequently asked

Common questions
about emergency lighting.

Quick answers to the things customers most often ask before booking a survey.

Do I legally need emergency lighting?

Yes for almost every business premises. The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 requires the responsible person to ensure escape routes are illuminated where necessary in the event of mains failure. The Building Regulations Approved Document B and BS 5266-1:2016 set the design standard. In practice that captures offices, shops, restaurants, schools, care homes, hotels, GP surgeries, multi-occupancy buildings, and almost any premises where the public or staff need to evacuate when the lights go out. Domestic-only single-family homes are usually exempt.

What's the difference between BS 5266 and BS 5839?

BS 5266-1 is the standard for emergency lighting — the battery-backed luminaires and EXIT signs that come on if the mains supply fails. BS 5839-1 is the standard for fire alarm and detection systems — panels, call points, sounders and detectors. They are two separate systems, two separate standards, and two separate certificates. We design and install both, and they are usually surveyed at the same time because the legal trigger (the fire risk assessment under the Fire Safety Order) is the same.

How often does emergency lighting need testing?

BS 5266-1 requires two routine tests. Monthly: a short function test where every luminaire is briefly switched to battery to confirm the lamp lights and the changeover circuit works. Annually: a full 3-hour duration discharge test where the system runs on battery only for the full rated duration, every fitting is observed at the start and end, and the result is logged. Both tests must be recorded in an emergency lighting logbook held with the fire risk assessment. We deliver both as a contracted service with certificates issued each visit.

Can I install emergency lighting myself?

No — for two reasons. Legally, emergency lighting is fixed wiring connected to the mains supply, so it falls under Part P of the Building Regulations and must be installed by a competent person registered with NAPIT, NICEIC or equivalent. Practically, BS 5266-1 design (lux levels on the escape route, sign placement, open-area anti-panic coverage, high-risk task area lighting) is a calculated discipline, not a guess. A self-installed system that fails on the night of a fire is the worst possible outcome — both for the people inside and for the responsible person under the Fire Safety Order.

Maintained or non-maintained — which do I need?

Maintained luminaires are always lit (used as normal lighting too) and stay lit on battery if mains fails. Used for EXIT signs in places of assembly (cinemas, theatres, restaurants, function rooms) where they must be visible at all times, and for escape route lighting in spaces that are normally dark or dimmed. Non-maintained luminaires are off in normal use and only switch on when mains supply to the local final circuit is lost. Used for escape route lighting in offices, warehouses and back-of-house corridors where general lighting is on during occupancy. Most premises end up with a mix — maintained EXIT signs plus non-maintained escape route luminaires. We design the layout per BS 5266-1 Section 5.
