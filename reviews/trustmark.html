What TrustMark means

Backed by Government. Vetted by industry.

TrustMark is operated under licence from the UK government's Department for Energy Security & Net Zero. Membership requires every listed contractor to meet three independently-checked standards before being allowed to display the logo.

01 / TECHNICAL

Technical competence

Members are checked annually against the technical standards of their trade. For electrical work, that means full Part P registration, BS 7671 compliance, and a competent-person scheme membership (in our case, NAPIT).

02 / CUSTOMER

Customer service

Quoting, contracts, complaint handling and dispute resolution are all checked against TrustMark's customer charter. Members agree to a published code of practice covering how they treat customers.

03 / TRADING

Trading standards

Identity, insurance and trading history are verified. Listed contractors are committed to fair trading, advertising honestly, and operating to consumer law — checked annually.

What we sign up to as a TrustMark contractor.

It's not just a logo on the van. To stay listed we agree, in writing, to a public code of conduct. Some of the things we commit to:

  • Honest quotes. Clear, written quotes — not "we'll let you know what it costs at the end".
  • Compliant work. Every installation to BS 7671 18th Edition, every job certified through NAPIT, every notifiable job registered for Part P building control.
  • Fair complaint handling. If something goes wrong, we deal with it ourselves first; if you're not happy, TrustMark provides an independent escalation route.
  • Insurance backing. Members maintain the public liability cover required for the work they undertake. We carry £2M.
  • Annual checks. Listed status isn't a one-off — TrustMark and our scheme operator (NAPIT) re-check us every year.
