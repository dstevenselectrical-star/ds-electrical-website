Local Knowledge · Bath

Why pub & restaurant electrical work in Bath is different

Bath's pub & restaurant scene is split between the heritage city centre — Pulteney Street, Milsom Street, Walcot, the Royal Crescent run — and the Bath Quays / Riverside / Lower Bristol Road newer-build food-and-drink cluster. The heritage core is mostly Grade I/II listed: concealed cable runs, no surface trunking, careful coordination with conservation officers, and gas-to-induction kitchen swaps now becoming routine as venues upgrade. The Quays cluster is modern-build but often shares landlord 3-phase distribution that has to be checked before anyone adds a 32A induction hob.

Coverage area: Royal Crescent, Pulteney, Milsom, Walcot, Bathwick, Larkhall, Oldfield Park, Twerton, Bath Quays, Lower Bristol Road, Locksbrook, Combe Down.

Sister cities: Bath · Wells · Frome · Glastonbury · Shepton Mallet · Bruton
