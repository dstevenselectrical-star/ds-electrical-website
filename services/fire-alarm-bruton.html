BS 5839 Categories Explained

  • Category L1 — Full property coverage. Every room protected. Required for care homes and high-risk premises
  • Category L2 — Escape routes plus high-risk rooms. Common for Bruton restaurants, galleries and commercial premises
  • Category L3 — Escape route protection only. Standard for most rental properties and landlord compliance
  • Category L4 — Circulation spaces only (hallways and landings). Minimum for single-occupancy dwellings

Conventional vs Addressable Systems

Smaller Bruton properties suit conventional zone-based panels at lower cost. For larger buildings such as restaurants, hotels and multi-unit commercial premises in Bruton, addressable systems identify the exact detector that triggered, reducing false alarm disruption and enabling targeted evacuation response.

FAQ

Common Questions

Can you install fire alarms at Bruton schools, hotels and galleries?

Yes. Bruton high-end commercial sites need Grade L1 systems (full coverage) with addressable panels for fault diagnostics. Hauser & Wirth, Bruton schools and hotels all need fire-alarm certificates for their insurance and Fire Risk Assessment. Annual service contract included.

What's the difference between BS 5839 Grade A, L1, L2, L3, M, P1?

Commercial categories: P1 = property protection (full coverage); P2 = property protection (high-risk areas only); L1 = life safety (full coverage including all rooms); L2 = life safety (escape routes + high-risk areas); L3 = life safety (escape routes only); M = manual call points only. Domestic grades A through F per BS 5839-6 — Grade D commonly required in HMOs and rentals.

Do you service fire alarms after install?

Yes. BS 5839-1 requires 6-monthly servicing for commercial systems — we offer a service contract (covers 2 visits per year, signed test certificate, battery replacement). For BS 5839-6 domestic systems annual user-test only is required, but we recommend a yearly engineer check-over especially in landlord properties.
