Local Knowledge · Frome

Why fleet EV in Frome needs a load assessment first

Frome's a different shape from Wells or Bath — the Marston Trading Estate and Commerce Park to the south carry most of the town's industrial and fleet operators, with strong 3-phase availability for 8 to 12-bay fleet rollouts without DNO escalation. The Cheap Street / Catherine Hill independent retail core is heritage and tight, so we usually recommend single 7 kW destination chargers with dynamic load management rather than upgrades. Frome has a high proportion of creative-sector and food-and-drink SMEs taking up the OZEV grant for staff parking.

Older 1970s units around Westway / Vallis Way frequently still have legacy split-load consumer units that don't meet BS 7671 18th Edition Amd 2 for new EV circuits — Type A RCBO is mandatory. Marston Trading Estate has good supply infrastructure but landlord consent for sub-main runs needs sorting upfront.
