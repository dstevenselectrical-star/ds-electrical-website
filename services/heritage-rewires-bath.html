What a heritage rewire in Bath covers

A heritage rewire isn't a normal rewire. Working in Georgian, Regency, Victorian and Edwardian stock in Bath means you can't chase walls, you can't drill through original cornice, and you can't bury cables under modern plasterboard tricks. Every cable route is planned through joist voids, behind original skirting, under floorboards or around chimney breasts. Lath-and-plaster walls get fished from above and below — never broken open. No surface trunking, ever. Brass plate accessories (Click Scolmore Definity / Forbes & Lomax / Hamilton — specifier-led) replace plastic. Dimming circuits installed throughout to match period lighting design. Full Listed Building Consent supporting documentation provided as standard.

FAQ

Heritage rewires in Bath: common questions

Do I need Listed Building Consent for a full rewire in Bath?

For Grade I, Grade II* and most Grade II Listed properties — yes for any full rewire. We help draft the supporting note describing cable routes, fixings, accessory style and reinstatement. The Bath & North East Somerset Council conservation team usually signs off straightforward heritage rewires within a few weeks; complex jobs may need a heritage statement.

How do you rewire lath-and-plaster walls without breaking them open?

We fish cables down through the void between lath and stud using rods, draw cords and access via floorboards above or skirting below. New back-boxes are cut neatly with a multitool — never with a chase grinder. Where a route is impossible without damage, we route via the floor void in the room above and drop in. Surface trunking is never used on heritage work.

What brass accessories do you fit on heritage rewires?

Specifier-led — we'll fit whatever the architect or interior designer has chosen. Click Scolmore Definity (antique brass / aged brass screwless) is our value default. Forbes & Lomax (Invisible Lightswitch, Nickel Silver, Antique Bronze) is our specifier-grade default. Hamilton Hartland CFX, Hartland G2 and Sheer ranges are also regular fits. We hold trade accounts with all three.

How long does a Bath heritage rewire take?

3-bed Victorian terrace: 8–14 working days on site. 4-bed period home: 14–22 working days. 5-bed Listed Georgian: 18–28 working days plus snagging. Phased works available where the property stays occupied. We always agree the programme in writing before first-fix.
