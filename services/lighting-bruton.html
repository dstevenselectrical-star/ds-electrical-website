Why lighting in Bruton is different

Bruton (BA10) is the high-end end of east Somerset — Hauser & Wirth Somerset, At The Chapel, the Newt nearby. Stock here is period stone (a lot of it Listed), barn conversions and high-spec country properties where the brief is usually 'invisible' wiring, hidden control, and finish detail that has to match conservation-grade work elsewhere on the property.

Bruton stock is heavily Listed and conservation-area: stone period buildings on the High Street, surrounding country properties, plus the gallery/hospitality estate.

Bruton work is finish-led — clients here expect the same standard of craft on the electrics as on the joinery and stonework. Hidden cable, surface-fix only where it has to be, fittings chosen to match the architectural period.

FAQ

Common questions

Can you do a full lighting redesign in Bruton — not just like-for-like?

Yes. Lighting design is one of the things we genuinely enjoy — circuits planned around how you actually use the room, dimming scenes that work day and night, fittings chosen to match the property and the brief. Bruton is a mix of period properties and modern stock, so we'll often combine recessed LED, pendants, wall lights and feature lighting on a single redesign. Full design and quote at survey.

Will LED downlights compromise fire compartmentation in my ceiling?

Not if installed correctly. Where the ceiling is a separating floor (between flats) or otherwise fire-rated, every downlight has to be installed inside a fire-rated hood that maintains the ceiling's 30 or 60-minute rating. We use named-product hoods (Tenmat, Tlc) and document the install on the EIC.

Can you retrofit smart lighting without rewiring?

Often, yes. Lutron Caseta, Shelly modules, Hue and similar smart-dimming systems retrofit into existing wiring without a neutral at the switch (in many cases). We'll survey the existing setup, advise what'll work and what won't, and quote on commissioning + app setup as part of the install.
