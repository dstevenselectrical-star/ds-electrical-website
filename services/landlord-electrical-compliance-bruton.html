Why Bruton is different

Bruton (BA10) is the village reshaped by Hauser & Wirth and the Newt — landlord stock is increasingly premium, and the EICR sits alongside high-end refurb work on most rentals. We fit the EICR and alarm pack between the visual refurb and the new tenant arriving.

BA10 covers Bruton itself plus Castle Cary, Wincanton, Pitcombe, Cucklington, Charlton Musgrove and the Newt-area cottages. The corridor has a lot of holiday-let-meets-long-let stock that needs EICR + Grade D smoke at change of tenancy.

Tenants in this corridor are typically high-spec and ask questions — we leave a tenant-friendly 'electrical safety summary' card on the consumer unit so the EICR validity, alarm test schedule and emergency contact are all visible.

FAQ

Common questions

Do you EICR rentals in Bruton, Castle Cary and the Newt area?

Yes — Bruton (BA10) including Hauser & Wirth's village footprint, the Newt-area cottages, Wincanton, Castle Cary and Pitcombe. A lot of rentals in this corridor have been refurb-let to high-end tenants, so the EICR needs to land alongside the visual refurb — we schedule first visit before the new lighting and second-fix to certify after.

Is a 5-year EICR legally required for a rental?

Yes. The Electrical Safety Standards in the Private Rented Sector (England) Regulations 2020 require an EICR every 5 years (and at change of tenant if the report is due). The certificate must be issued by a competent person — we're NAPIT-registered (66245) and the report is admissible by every UK letting agent and council.

What smoke alarms do I legally need?

For England the minimum is one mains-wired smoke alarm on every storey of the let property and a heat alarm in every kitchen — the regs sit alongside BS 5839-6 Grade D. We fit interlinked Grade D pack as standard so a downstairs alarm trips the upstairs unit, and we always issue a BS 5839-6 install certificate on the day.
