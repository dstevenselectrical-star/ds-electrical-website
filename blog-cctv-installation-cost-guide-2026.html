CCTV Installation Cost Guide 2026

What Mid Somerset homeowners actually pay for a professionally installed Hikvision 4K system — from a 2-camera starter to full property coverage. Real prices, no guesswork.

By DS Electrical · 23 April 2026 · 7 min read

Online CCTV prices are all over the place. A quick search throws up cheap DIY kits for £150 and "professional installations" for £3,000 with no explanation of the difference. This guide cuts through that with the actual prices we charge here in Mid Somerset for professionally installed Hikvision 4K AcuSense systems — everything included, no hidden extras.

What Drives the Cost Up (or Down)?

Two jobs with the same number of cameras can have very different prices. Here is what actually moves the needle:

Cable routing difficulty

This is the biggest variable. Running Cat6 cable through a modern cavity-wall new-build is straightforward. Running it through a 200-year-old stone farmhouse with 600mm walls, timber-frame ceilings, and no loft access is a completely different job. Properties in Castle Cary, Bruton, and the older parts of Frome often fall into the latter category. Expect the top end of any price band if your property has complex masonry or limited internal routing options.

NVR storage specification

Every system includes a Network Video Recorder (NVR). A 4-camera system typically comes with a 4TB drive, giving you 2–4 weeks of continuous footage at 4K depending on camera count and motion settings. If you want 8TB for longer retention or higher-resolution recording, the NVR cost increases accordingly. We discuss storage requirements with every customer before quoting.

PoE switch size

Cameras run over Power over Ethernet (PoE), meaning a single Cat6 cable carries both data and power. For systems up to 4 cameras, a dedicated NVR with built-in PoE ports handles everything. Larger systems (6–8 cameras, or commercial sites) typically need a separate managed PoE switch, which adds hardware cost and some additional configuration time.

Remote access and network setup

Every installation includes setup of Hik-Connect, Hikvision’s free remote viewing app, so you can watch live footage and review recordings from your phone anywhere in the world. This is straightforward on most home networks. If your broadband router has restrictive port settings or you want a more advanced configuration (VPN access, separate IoT VLAN), allow for some additional time.

Hik-Connect: included on every install

We configure Hik-Connect on every job at no extra charge. Once set up, you get live view, playback, and motion alerts on iOS or Android. Most customers are watching their property within minutes of us leaving. There are no ongoing subscription fees — the app and P2P cloud relay are free.

Breaking Down Each Tier

2-Camera Starter System — £650–£850

A two-camera setup is ideal for a flat, terraced house, or cottage where you want front door and rear garden coverage. We typically use two Hikvision 4K AcuSense bullet cameras paired with a compact 4-channel NVR. AcuSense technology uses deep-learning to distinguish people and vehicles from animals or foliage, so you get meaningful alerts rather than false triggers from passing cats.

The lower end of this range (around £650) applies to properties with easy cable routing — cavity walls, accessible loft, cameras close to the NVR location. The upper end (£850) reflects longer cable runs, surface-mounted trunking on a hard material, or a second floor camera position requiring a longer drop.

4-Camera Standard System — £1,200–£1,500

This is the most popular choice for a typical semi-detached or detached home in Wells, Shepton Mallet, or Midsomer Norton. Four cameras give you coverage of all four elevations — front driveway, rear garden, and both side accesses. A 4-channel NVR with 4TB storage provides ample retention. Most families find four cameras sufficient to remove all blind spots around a standard domestic property.

6–8 Camera Full Coverage — £1,800–£2,800

Larger detached properties, those with outbuildings, garages, or extended driveways, or any property where perimeter monitoring is a priority benefit from a six to eight camera system. The wider price range here reflects the increased variability in cable routing complexity and NVR specification. An 8-camera system with an 8TB NVR on a straightforward new-build will sit towards £1,800. The same camera count on a rural property near Bath or Frome with stone outbuildings, exposed soffits, and a long cable run to a detached garage will naturally push towards £2,800.

Commercial and PTZ Systems — Quote Per Site

Commercial premises, car parks, retail units, and sites requiring pan-tilt-zoom (PTZ) cameras or multi-head turret arrays are quoted on a per-site basis. Variables include the number of cameras, whether cable infrastructure already exists, type of NVR or VMS required, and integration with existing access control or alarm systems. We work across Wells, Bath, Shepton Mallet, and the wider Mid Somerset commercial area — contact us to arrange a free site survey.

What You Need at Home Before We Install

A CCTV system needs two things from your property: a 240V mains socket near the NVR location (usually in a utility room, loft, or comms cupboard), and a network connection to the same NVR if you want remote viewing via Hik-Connect. Most homes already have a suitable socket and router nearby. If neither is available where the NVR needs to go, we can add a spur from an existing circuit as part of the same job — this is typically an additional £80–£120.

You do not need to supply Cat6 cable, drill anything, or have any existing infrastructure in place. We bring all materials and leave the property clean and tidy.

Domestic vs Commercial Grade: What’s the Difference?

The Hikvision AcuSense range we install is the same hardware used by commercial installers — there is no "consumer" downgrade. The distinction between a domestic and commercial job is usually about scale and infrastructure rather than camera quality.

A residential install typically involves 2–8 cameras, Cat6 cable concealed through the fabric of the building, and a standalone NVR in a utility room. A commercial install might involve 8–64 cameras, structured cabling through containment, a rack-mounted NVR or VMS server, UPS backup power, and integration with other systems. Both use the same 4K AcuSense cameras and the same Hikvision ecosystem. Our customer reviews reflect the same standard of finish across both.
