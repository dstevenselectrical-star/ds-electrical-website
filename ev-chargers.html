OZEV Grants

Up to £350 Off Domestic — Up to £14,000 for Workplace

EV Chargepoint Grant (Domestic): Up to £350 towards the cost of installing a home charger. Available to homeowners, tenants, and landlords with eligible off-street parking. The property must not have previously received a grant under the EVHS or OZEV scheme.

Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS): Businesses, charities, and public sector organisations can claim up to £350 per socket, for up to 40 sockets per applicant — that is up to £14,000 towards your workplace charging infrastructure. Eligible for any business with dedicated off-street parking.

We handle the full application process for both schemes. For domestic, you need:

  • Proof of EV ownership or order confirmation (plug-in hybrid or fully electric)
  • Off-street parking (driveway, garage, or dedicated space)
  • Property ownership or landlord/management company consent

For the Workplace Charging Scheme, we handle all paperwork including the OZEV online portal application, installer declaration, and post-installation evidence pack with photographs and certification.

Single-Phase vs Three-Phase

Understanding Charging Speeds

Single-phase (230V, most UK homes): Maximum 7.4kW charging speed. Adds approximately 25-30 miles of range per hour. A typical EV (60kWh battery) charges from 20% to 80% in around 5 hours. More than sufficient for overnight home charging.

Three-phase (400V, commercial & some larger homes): Up to 22kW charging speed. Adds approximately 75-90 miles of range per hour. Ideal for workplaces, fleet depots, hotels, and destinations where faster turnaround is needed. Requires a three-phase supply — we can arrange upgrades through your DNO if required.

Key consideration: Your charging speed is limited by either the charger output OR the vehicle's onboard charger — whichever is lower. Most EVs accept 7kW AC; some (like Tesla Model 3 and BMW i4) accept 11kW; fewer accept the full 22kW. We advise on the right charger for your specific vehicle.

Both domestic and commercial installations require a site survey to confirm cable routes, supply capacity, main fuse rating, and earthing arrangements. We provide free no-obligation surveys across our coverage area.

Technical Requirements

Installation
Engineering

EV chargers draw sustained high current — typically 32A continuous for hours. A properly designed dedicated circuit is non-negotiable for safety, performance, and compliance with BS 7671:2018+A2 Section 722.

01

32A Radial Circuit

6mm² twin-and-earth for internal runs up to 22m, or 10mm² SWA cable for longer external runs and underground routes. Cable sizing calculated per BS 7671 Appendix 4, accounting for volt drop (max 5%), grouping factors, thermal insulation, and ambient temperature. We never undersize cables.

02

RCD Protection & DC Fault Detection

BS 7671 Regulation 722.531.2 requires Type A RCD protection with additional 6mA DC residual current detection for EV charging circuits. The chargers we install (Hypervolt 3, Ohme, Zappi, Easee, Andersen) all have integral 6mA DC fault detection, so a Type A RCBO at the consumer unit is all that's required. No additional DC module or upgraded device needed.

03

DNO Notification

All EV charger installations above 3.68kW require notification to your Distribution Network Operator per the Electricity Safety, Quality and Continuity Regulations 2002. Installations above 7kW (or multiple chargers) may require a supply capacity assessment, and in some cases a main fuse upgrade from 60A to 100A. We handle all DNO correspondence and applications.

04

Supply Capacity Assessment

We measure your existing demand and verify your main fuse rating (typically 60A, 80A, or 100A). A 7kW charger draws 32A — if your existing peak demand is 40A on a 60A supply, there is a problem. We model your load profile and recommend solutions: supply upgrade, smart load management via CT clamp, or scheduled off-peak charging to stay within your capacity.

05

PME Earthing Considerations

Most UK homes have PME (TN-C-S) earthing. BS 7671 Regulation 722.411.4.1 requires additional protective measures for EV charging on PME supplies due to the risk of a PEN conductor fault. Options include: chargers with integral PEN fault detection (Andersen, some Easee units), installation of an earth rod to create a TT arrangement at the charger, or a separate TT earth electrode. We assess your earthing and implement the correct solution.

06

Load Management

For properties with multiple chargers (or limited supply capacity), we install CT clamp-based dynamic load management. This monitors real-time household demand and throttles the charger output to prevent main fuse overload. Easee supports load balancing across up to 3 chargers natively. For larger installations, we configure OCPP-based load management systems.

Common Questions

EV Charger FAQ

How much does a home EV charger cost to install?+

A home EV charger installed by DS Electrical starts from around £850, which includes the charger unit, dedicated 32A circuit from your consumer unit, all cabling, and NAPIT certification. The exact price depends on the distance from your consumer unit to the charging location and the charger brand you choose.

Which EV charger brand is best?+

It depends on your needs. Hypervolt 3 is our most popular for reliability and smart tariff integration. Easee is the most compact and aesthetically clean. Ohme offers the best smart charging for Octopus Go users. Zappi is strong for anyone in the myenergi ecosystem. We install all major brands and can advise which suits your setup.

Do I need to upgrade my consumer unit for an EV charger?+

Not always, but often yes. EV chargers require a Type A RCD with 6mA DC detection, which older consumer units don't have. We'll survey your board during the site visit and include any upgrade in the fixed-price quote. A consumer unit upgrade adds around £350–£650 depending on the board.

How long does EV charger installation take?+

A standard installation takes 3–4 hours. If the cable run is longer or a consumer unit upgrade is needed, it may take a full day. We aim to book installations within 1–2 weeks of your survey.

Is there still a government grant for EV chargers?+

The OZEV grant for homeowners ended in March 2024. However, grants of up to £350 are still available for landlords and tenants, and the Workplace Charging Scheme offers up to £350 per socket for businesses. We handle all the grant paperwork.
