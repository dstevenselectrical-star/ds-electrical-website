Local Knowledge · Glastonbury

Why school work in Glastonbury is different

Glastonbury is our home base — we drive past every school in the town on our way to and from the unit. The schools here sit on a mix of 1960s flat-roof builds and older stone primaries — both have aged distribution boards, fire-alarm panels well past their service life, and emergency lighting that fails BS 5266 the moment you put a multimeter on it. We replace boards, fit Cat L3 addressable fire alarms, and certify the lot.

Local advantage matters: response time on emergency callouts (failed fire alarm panel, tripped distribution board mid-term) is under 15 minutes from Glastonbury through to Edgarley and Butleigh Wootton. Half-term and holiday windows are when we do the heavy work.
