Cabinet Sizes

From a 6U on the wall
to a 42U on castors.

We size the cabinet to the kit plus 25% headroom — never tight. Wall-mount up to about 12U where weight allows; floor-standing from 18U up. Full 19-inch rack mount with adjustable rails, lockable front and side panels, vented for passive cooling.

How We Build It

Build it for the sparky
walking in five years from now.

A network cabinet that’s a tip costs you money on every callout for the rest of its life. We dress and label every cabinet so the next person who opens the door — whether that’s us, your IT team, or another contractor — can trace any port in seconds.

01 Sized With Headroom

Cabinet U-count = current kit + 25% growth. We never spec a 9U cabinet that’s already 8U full on day one — that’s a rebuild waiting to happen. Same for PDU outlets, switch ports and UPS capacity.

02 Dressed, Not Stuffed

Vertical cable managers down both sides, horizontal finger-ducts between every patch panel and switch, service loops sized to allow movement without strain. Velcro at fixed intervals — never zip-ties, which kink the cable and break NEXT performance.

03 Labelled Both Ends

Brother / Brady industrial labels on every patch panel port AND every wall plate / floor box at the other end. Numbers match the cable tester test sheet, so any port can be traced from either end without a toner.

04 Power Done Properly

Switched, metered PDU at the top. UPS at the bottom (heaviest item, lowest centre of gravity). Surge protection on the PDU input. Power load balanced across UPS phases for 3-phase rooms.

05 Earthed & Bonded

Dedicated 6 mm² protective conductor from the cabinet earth bar back to the main electrical earth, sized for the full fault current. Every door, side panel and removable section bonded back to the frame with braided straps. BS 7671 + IEEE compliant.

06 Documented at Handover

You get a PDF pack: the patch schedule (port to wall plate to room), the test report per port, the rack elevation diagram showing what’s in which U, the IP plan, the credentials list. Hand it to the next contractor in five years — it still makes sense.

07 Existing Cabinet Rebuilds

We take messy existing cabinets apart, document what was there, re-cable as needed, replace patch panels if they’re beyond saving, dress and label everything to spec, and hand back a clean test pack. Always done out of hours for sensitive sites.

08 Lockable & Secure

Front, side and (on floor cabinets) rear doors lockable as standard. For PCI / ICO-sensitive environments we add cage locks, an internal dome camera in the cabinet, and door-open sensors wired to the alarm panel.
