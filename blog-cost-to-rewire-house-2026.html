How Much Does It Cost to Rewire a House in 2026?

Real 2026 pricing from a NAPIT approved Mid Somerset electrical contractor. No ranges hidden behind "get a quote" — here's what you should actually expect to pay.

By DS Electrical · April 2026 · 7 min read

The question we get asked more than any other: how much will my rewire cost? The honest answer is that it depends on your property, but a fixed-price quote should be possible within 48 hours of a free site survey. This guide covers what drives the price, what a realistic 2026 budget looks like, and what’s included.

Quick answer for 2026: 2-bed flat from £3,500. 3-bed semi from £4,500. 4-bed detached from £6,500. Full commercial or listed building is priced individually after survey.

What a rewire actually includes

A full rewire replaces every cable in your property. Walk into any electrician’s van and the list will be similar:

  • New consumer unit (fuseboard) with RCBO protection on every circuit and surge protection (SPD) as standard, compliant with BS 7671:2018 Amendment 2
  • New ring mains in 2.5mm² T&E for socket circuits
  • New lighting circuits in 1.5mm² T&E with dedicated neutrals for every switch
  • New high-load circuits for the cooker, shower, and EV charger (if fitted)
  • New smoke and heat detection to BS 5839-6 (Grade D interlinked mains with 10-year battery backup)
  • New earthing and bonding including main protective bonding to incoming gas and water services
  • Full BS 7671 testing and certification with an Electrical Installation Certificate issued on completion
  • NAPIT Part P notification lodged with your local authority on your behalf

What pushes the price up

1. Solid walls and stone construction

Plasterboard and timber studwork wall construction is straightforward: we drill, fish cables, and patch. Solid brick, ashlar stone, or random rubble Mendip stone walls need chasing (cutting channels into the wall). That adds labour and means your plasterer has more work after we leave.

2. Solid concrete floors

Suspended timber floors let us run cables under floorboards quickly. Solid concrete floors mean running cables overhead into every room from the ceiling, or chasing into walls at skirting level. Both add time.

3. Property is occupied during works

Empty properties are 20% faster and cheaper. Working around your furniture, covering floors and sheets every day, and maintaining power to essential areas (freezer, Wi-Fi, lighting) while rewiring adds meaningful time.

4. Listed buildings and conservation areas

Listed Building Consent may be required for any visible fitting or chase. Conservation officer liaison, additional documentation, and the need to use sensitive routing (often surface conduit painted to match) can add 15–25% to the project cost.

5. Additional works alongside

If you’re rewiring, it makes sense to do EV charger first-fix (even if not commissioned yet), data cabling (Cat6), structured lighting with scene control, or an additional CCTV first-fix. These add cost but save you a second visit and second disruption later.

Partial rewires

A full rewire isn’t always needed. If your property has a modern RCD-protected consumer unit but older upstairs wiring, we can do a partial rewire:

  • Rewire just the first floor, or just the ground floor
  • Replace only the lighting circuits (leaving sockets)
  • Replace only the sockets (leaving lighting)
  • Rewire kitchen and bathroom only

Partial rewires typically run 40–60% of a full rewire price depending on scope. They also require a post-works EICR to confirm the remaining original installation is still safe.

Signs you need a rewire now

  • Cables with rubber, lead-sheathed, or fabric insulation (common pre-1960, sometimes up to 1975)
  • A fuseboard with rewirable fuses (no RCD, no MCBs)
  • Round pin sockets or braided/fabric-covered flex on light fittings
  • No earthing on lighting circuits (two-core twin-and-earth was standard after 1966 — earlier installations often have no CPC)
  • Frequent RCD trips, warm sockets, or the smell of burning plastic
  • An Unsatisfactory EICR result

What’s NOT included in a rewire quote (usually)

  • Plastering repair — we fill chases to a "closed" state but final plaster skim is usually done by your plasterer
  • Decorating — painting after plastering is a separate trade
  • Light fittings — we fit whatever you supply, so fitting prices vary wildly. Budget separately for these
  • Kitchen appliances — hob, oven, extractor connections are included; the appliances are not
  • Furniture / flooring moving — we lift carpets to access floorboards; replacing flooring after is usually excluded

We’ll always itemise inclusions and exclusions on your written quote so there are no surprises.
