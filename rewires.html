How It Works

Four steps from
quote to certificate.

Here's exactly what happens from your first call to the certificate landing in your inbox.

Step 01

Free on-site survey

We visit, look at the consumer unit, count circuits, check accessibility (lofts, voids, floors), discuss what stays and what goes. About one hour. No charge, no obligation.

Step 02

Fixed written quote

You get a written quote within 48 hours. Itemised: cable, accessories, consumer unit, labour days, certificate. Valid 30 days. 50% deposit on acceptance.

Step 03

First fix & second fix

Strip-out, cable runs, back-boxes, consumer unit installed and energised circuit-by-circuit. You stay in the property; only the active room loses power at any time.

Step 04

Test & certificate

Full BS 7671 initial verification: continuity, insulation resistance, earth loop impedance, RCD trip times. Electrical Installation Certificate emailed within 48 hours, Part P notification lodged with council automatically.

Common Questions

Everything people
ask before booking.

Straight answers to the questions that come up at survey. If we haven't covered yours below, ring us and ask — we won't charge for the answer.

How much does a house rewire cost in Somerset?

Typical 2026 prices: 2-bed terrace from £3,500, 3-bed semi from £4,500, 4-bed detached from £6,500, 5-bed plus from £10,000. Final price comes after a free on-site survey - no quote over the phone, every property is different. All prices include a new consumer unit, materials, testing and certificate (excluding VAT).

How long does a full house rewire take?

5 to 10 working days for a 3-bed semi. Smaller flats 3-4 days; 4-5 bed houses 8-14 days; heritage and listed properties 2-3 weeks. We work circuit-by-circuit so most of the property stays live throughout - usually only the active room is off.

Do I need to move out for a rewire?

Usually no. We work circuit-by-circuit so kitchen and at least one socket per floor stays live. Most clients stay in the house. Heritage properties with extensive plaster work are the exception - furniture protection becomes the bigger logistics job and we'll discuss that at survey.

Will the rewire damage the walls?

Some chasing is unavoidable. We minimise it with surface trunking where the room finish suits it, floorboard lifts upstairs, and chase-and-make-good on plastered walls. Small chases get filled and skimmed as standard. Full re-skim or replaster is a separate trade we can recommend.

What certificates do I get?

Electrical Installation Certificate (EIC) covering the full installation tested to BS 7671:2018+A2:2022. Part P Building Regulations notification automatically lodged with your local authority through our NAPIT registration (66245). Plus a digital photo record of cable routes and final consumer unit state, retained on file for any future works.

Can I have a partial rewire instead of a full one?

Yes - very common. Kitchen and bathroom only, ground-floor only, lighting only, single circuit. We're honest at survey about what's worth doing now versus what can wait. An EICR (from £200) tells us exactly which circuits are non-compliant - we can roll the EICR cost into the rewire quote if you proceed.

How do I know if I need a rewire at all?

Tell-tale signs: rewireable (Wylex BS 3036) fuse box, fabric or rubber-insulated cables visible at the consumer unit, two-pin sockets, no earth wire visible at switches or sockets, lights flicker when appliances start, RCDs trip frequently, burning smells from outlets. Free survey is the fastest answer; an EICR confirms it formally.

Are you registered for Building Regs Part P?

Yes. NAPIT membership 66245 is a Competent Person Scheme registered with the Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities. We notify your local authority Building Control automatically on completion - no separate paperwork or fee.

Do you take a deposit?

Yes - 50% deposit on acceptance of the quote (covers materials and first-fix labour), balance due immediately on completion of works and issue of certificate. Payment by BACS only. Bank details are on every invoice.

Are you NAPIT or NICEIC?

NAPIT - Approved Contractor 66245, active until 28 February 2027. NAPIT and NICEIC are both Competent Person Schemes recognised under Part P. Both notify councils, both audit annually, both certificates carry the same legal weight.
