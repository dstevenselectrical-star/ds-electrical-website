Electrical Safety Checklist —
UK Homes & Rentals.

10-minute self-check. If anything fails, book an EICR before your next renewal.

Who this is for

Landlords, homeowners, anyone overdue an EICR.

This page is a free 10-minute walk-round you can do yourself before booking a professional inspection. It is for:

  • Landlords with an active tenancy — legal duty since 2020 to hold a current EICR every 5 years.
  • Homeowners pre-purchase — quick triage before instructing a survey or paying for an EICR.
  • Anyone with a property over 10 years since their last EICR — standards have moved on; your installation may not have.

What you’ll need

Five free minutes, a phone for photos of anything that looks wrong, and your meter cupboard key. That’s it.

Important: this checklist is a visual self-check, not a formal inspection. If anything below fails, do not attempt to fix it yourself — book an EICR with a 2391-qualified electrician.

A / Smoke Alarms (BS 5839-6)

Test every alarm.

Press the test button on every smoke and heat alarm in the property. The alarm should sound loudly and consistently.

  • Every alarm sounds when you press TEST.
  • No alarm is intermittently beeping (low battery on a 10-year sealed unit means the whole unit is end-of-life and must be replaced — you cannot replace the battery).
  • At least one mains-powered, interlinked smoke alarm per floor.
Standard: Scotland made interlinked alarms mandatory in February 2022 (Tolerable Standard). England and Wales: strongly recommended; mandatory in rental housing under the Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarm (England) Regulations 2022.
B / RCD Test (do this every 6 months)

Press T on every RCD.

Find your consumer unit (the “fuse board” — usually under the stairs, in the meter cupboard, or in the garage). On each RCD or RCBO, press the small button marked T or TEST.

  • Every RCD trips immediately when tested.
  • You can reset each RCD by switching it back up.
  • No RCD requires more than one push to trip.
Failed? A non-tripping RCD means you have no shock protection on that circuit. Stop using it and call an electrician the same day.
C / Visible Fuse Board Check

Modern board, or upgrade?

Look at your consumer unit (do not open it). Ask yourself:

  • Modern (good): metal-cased enclosure, individual white RCBO switches, each with its own TEST button. Compliant with BS 7671 18th edition.
  • Old plastic body (pre-2018): all-plastic enclosure — needs upgrading to a metal board for fire-containment compliance.
  • Rewireable fuses (BS 3036): screw-in porcelain holders with fuse wire — upgrade urgent. No RCD protection means no shock protection.
Useful: see our consumer-unit upgrade page for fixed pricing on metal-cased boards (RCBO per circuit, full Part P certification).
D / Sockets & Switches

Walk the property. Look closely.

Every socket and switch in the property gets a quick visual check.

  • No cracks across the face-plate or surround.
  • No black scorch marks above or around any socket.
  • No socket feels warm or hot to the back of your hand (a working socket should be ambient temperature, even with a kettle plugged in).
  • No loose face-plates that move when you plug something in.
  • No socket has visible burning smell when in use.
Any scorch marks? Stop using that socket immediately. Scorching is evidence of arcing — the next stage is fire. Photograph it and call an electrician today.
E / Bathroom (BS 7671 Section 701)

Special-location rules apply.

Bathrooms have their own zoned wiring rules because water and electricity do not mix. Check the following:

  • No standard 13A sockets inside zone 1 or zone 2 (anywhere within 0.6m of bath or shower).
  • Light switches inside the bathroom are pull-cord type only. Wall switches must be mounted outside.
  • Shaver socket only acceptable if it has an isolating transformer (the unit will be marked).
  • Light fittings and extractor fans inside the zones are IP44 minimum (sealed against water spray).
  • If a heated mirror, towel rail, or underfloor heating is fitted — certificate should exist.
F / Outdoor & Garden

Outside circuits get tougher rules.

  • Every outdoor socket is RCD-protected (check the consumer unit or look for a TEST button on the socket itself).
  • Buried cables are SWA (steel-wire armoured) — never standard twin-and-earth.
  • External light fittings are IP44 or higher (IP65 recommended for fully exposed).
  • Hot tubs, swim-spas, and outdoor kitchens are on a dedicated circuit with Type A RCBO (mandatory under BS 7671 18th edition Amendment 2).
  • No extension leads buried, run through walls, or used outside in wet conditions.
G / EV Charger (if installed)

Type A RCBO with DC fault detection.

EV chargers fitted from 2020 onwards must use a Type A RCBO with DC fault detection (or equivalent inverter-side protection). DS Electrical fits Type A on every install.

  • Charger fitted by a certified installer (you should have an Electrical Installation Certificate).
  • Charger has its own dedicated circuit from the consumer unit.
  • RCBO is Type A, never Type AC (Type AC misses DC fault current and is non-compliant for EV).
  • Earthing arrangement was specified at install (PME-isolation device or earth rod).
Pre-2020 charger? May not be 18th edition compliant. Have it checked — we can certify or upgrade existing units.
H / EICR Due Date

When is your next inspection due?

Find your last EICR certificate. Look at the “recommended date for next inspection” field. Compare it with today.

  • Domestic owner-occupied: every 10 years, or at change of occupancy — whichever is sooner.
  • Rental property: every 5 years (landlord legal duty under the Electrical Safety Standards in the Private Rented Sector Regulations 2020). Must be supplied to every new tenant.
  • Commercial/industrial: typically every 5 years; some heavy-use sites (commercial kitchens, workshops) every 3 years.
  • No EICR on file? Book one. Default position is overdue.
EICR expired? For rentals this is a legal breach with civil penalties up to £30,000. Book the new inspection now.
Anything failed?

Book an EICR.
